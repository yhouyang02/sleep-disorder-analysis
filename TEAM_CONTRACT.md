
 Teamwork Contract

## 1. Expectations & Workload
- **Time Commitment:** The expected time for group members to work on the project will be approximately **5 hours per person per week**.
- **Workflow:** Work distribution will take place every **Monday** immediately following the class session.
- **Delays:** If a team member anticipates they cannot complete their sections within the expected hours, they must provide notice on Slack as soon as possible to facilitate work redistribution.

## 2. Meetings
Attendance is expected for scheduled meetings. If a member cannot attend, they must notify the team in advance.
- **Monday (Weekly Planning):** Held after class (Location: TBD). Focus: Work distribution.
- **Tuesday (Check-in):** 2:00 PM during the Lab session.
- **Saturday (Pre-submission):** Optional meetings held via Zoom prior to the 6:00 PM submission deadline.

## 3. Communication
- **Official Channel (Project & Analysis):** **GitHub** will be the primary communication tool for project analysis, ideas, and reviewing work. All major decisions and technical discussions should be tracked here.
- **Coordination Channel:** **Slack** will be used only for informal communication, scheduling, and quick coordination.
- **Daily Updates:** All members are required to post a daily update on Slack reporting their planned work for that day.
- **Meeting Notes:** A summary of decisions made during meetings must be documented. This task will be **rotated** among members. The assigned note-taker is responsible for posting the notes as a **GitHub Issue** on the team repository.

## 4. Availability & Constraints
Team members will respect the following availability constraints when scheduling ad-hoc meetings or expecting responses:
- **Yuheng & Norton:** Unavailable on Sundays.
- **Harpreet:** Unavailable for *in-person* collaboration from Friday through Sunday.
- **Eduardo:** Unavailable on Fridays from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM (16:00).

## 5. Deadlines
- **Project Submission:** The hard deadline for submissions is **Saturday at 6:00 PM**.
- Team members should aim to have their individual contributions ready well before this time to allow for integration and final checks during the optional Saturday Zoom session.

## 6. Breach of Contract
*Adapted from DSCI 524 Sample Contract:*
Failure to follow the team contract will result in a three-step warning system:
1. **Written Notice:** A Slack message outlining the violation and a reminder of the contract conditions.
2. **Meeting:** A discussion to address the behavior and draft a written agreement to adhere to the contract.
3. **Escalation:** Reaching out to the lab instructor or TAs regarding the issue.
